This comparison examines the practical differences to help you decide which platform aligns with your goals.

## Pricing

### Invoca Pricing

Invoca does not publish pricing on its website. Contracts are negotiated through a sales process and typically involve annual commitments with minimum call volumes. Industry estimates place Invoca's starting cost in the range of $1,000 to $2,000 per month, with enterprise deployments often exceeding $5,000 per month. The sales-driven pricing model reflects Invoca's enterprise positioning but creates uncertainty for mid-market buyers trying to evaluate total cost of ownership before committing.

### VeloCalls Pricing

VeloCalls publishes transparent, tiered pricing on its website. Plans scale based on call volume, and core features are included at every level. There are no mandatory annual contracts for most tiers, and businesses can start with a 14-day free trial to validate the platform before spending anything. For teams processing high call volumes, VeloCalls' usage-based model often delivers a significantly lower per-call cost compared to Invoca's enterprise pricing.

## AI Conversation Intelligence

Both platforms invest heavily in AI-powered call analysis, but their approaches differ.

### Invoca Signal AI

Invoca's Signal AI is its flagship product. It uses machine learning to classify call outcomes, detect conversion events, and extract structured data from conversations. Signal AI can identify whether a caller made a purchase, scheduled an appointment, or expressed dissatisfaction. The platform trains custom AI models on your specific call data, which improves accuracy over time. Invoca's strength here is its deep investment in pre-built AI models for specific verticals like automotive, healthcare, and financial services.

### VeloCalls AI Transcription and Scoring

VeloCalls provides AI transcription with sentiment analysis, intent detection, keyword spotting, and automatic call scoring. The platform analyzes calls in real time, identifying conversion signals and compliance risks as conversations happen. While VeloCalls may not have as many pre-built vertical-specific models as Invoca, its AI capabilities cover the core use cases that most businesses need: understanding call quality, detecting buyer intent, flagging compliance issues, and scoring calls for optimization.

## Call Attribution and Marketing Analytics

### Invoca Attribution

Invoca excels at connecting phone calls to digital marketing touchpoints. Its integrations with Google Ads, Adobe Analytics, and Salesforce are deeply built, supporting offline conversion imports, closed-loop attribution, and multi-touch models. For enterprise brands running large-scale media campaigns, Invoca's attribution engine is a proven solution. The platform also supports custom attribution models for businesses with unique buying cycles.

### VeloCalls Attribution

VeloCalls provides source-level, keyword-level, and publisher-level attribution. Dynamic number insertion tracks web sessions to phone calls, and integration with Google Ads and major analytics platforms enables standard marketing attribution. Where VeloCalls distinguishes itself is in pay-per-call attribution: tracking performance at the publisher, sub-ID, and campaign level with real-time revenue data. For performance marketers who need to attribute calls to specific affiliates and calculate real-time ROI, VeloCalls provides granular attribution that Invoca does not prioritize.

## IVR and Call Routing

### Invoca Routing

Invoca offers intelligent call routing based on Signal AI classifications, marketing source data, and caller attributes. Its routing can direct callers to specific agents or locations based on what the AI determines about the caller's intent or value. This is powerful for large contact centers that want to match high-value callers with their best agents.

### VeloCalls Routing

VeloCalls provides a visual, drag-and-drop IVR builder that lets you design complex call flows with conditional logic, time-based routing, geo-routing, whisper messages, and real-time buyer availability checks. The visual canvas makes it easy to build, test, and iterate on call flows without technical expertise. For pay-per-call operations that need to qualify callers through IVR before routing to the highest-bidding buyer, VeloCalls' routing engine is purpose-built for this workflow. ## Pay-Per-Call Marketplace

This represents the most significant functional difference between the two platforms.

**Invoca** does not offer a native pay-per-call marketplace. It works with partner networks and agencies through its partner program, but there is no built-in infrastructure for publisher management, real-time bidding, or automated payout calculation. Businesses that want to run pay-per-call through Invoca need to bolt on third-party tools or build custom integrations.

**VeloCalls** includes a full-featured marketplace with real-time bidding, publisher management, fraud detection, progressive KYC verification, and automated financial reconciliation. Publishers and buyers connect directly on the platform, with transparent bidding and performance analytics for both sides. If monetizing inbound calls through a marketplace is part of your business model, VeloCalls provides this infrastructure natively.

## Enterprise Features

### Invoca Enterprise Capabilities

Invoca has years of enterprise deployment experience. Its platform supports complex organizational hierarchies, granular user permissions, SSO/SAML integration, dedicated account management, and service-level agreements. The platform's Salesforce integration is particularly deep, supporting bi-directional data sync, custom object mapping, and revenue attribution within Salesforce's reporting engine. For organizations already invested in Salesforce as their system of record, Invoca fits naturally.

### VeloCalls Enterprise Capabilities

VeloCalls supports multi-tenant organization management, role-based access control, SSO/SAML authentication, API key management, IP whitelisting, and comprehensive audit logging. While VeloCalls' enterprise feature set is newer than Invoca's, it was architected from day one for multi-tenant isolation and API-first extensibility. The platform integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, and 40+ other platforms through native connectors and a documented REST API.
